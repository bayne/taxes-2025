# Notes on Title 26 (Internal Revenue Code) — Federal Income Tax Filing

These notes cover the portions of `title26.md` (U.S. Code Title 26) most relevant to filing federal individual income taxes. Line references are to `title26.md`.

---

## 1. Tax Computation Framework

### §1 — Tax Imposed (title26.md:5292)

Post-2017 rates (subsection (j), made permanent by Pub. L. 119-21): **7 brackets at 10%, 12%, 22%, 24%, 32%, 35%, 37%**

**Married Filing Jointly / Surviving Spouse** (2018 base amounts) (title26.md:6120):

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| $0 – $19,050 | 10% |
| $19,050 – $77,400 | 12% |
| $77,400 – $165,000 | 22% |
| $165,000 – $315,000 | 24% |
| $315,000 – $400,000 | 32% |
| $400,000 – $600,000 | 35% |
| Over $600,000 | 37% |

**Head of Household** (title26.md:6137): 10% up to $13,600; 12% to $51,800; 22% to $82,500; 24% to $157,500; 32% to $200,000; 35% to $500,000; 37% above

**Single** (title26.md:6154): 10% up to $9,525; 12% to $38,700; 22% to $82,500; 24% to $157,500; 32% to $200,000; 35% to $500,000; 37% above

**Married Filing Separately** (title26.md:6171): Generally half of MFJ amounts; 37% kicks in at $300,000

**Estates and Trusts** (title26.md:6188): Compressed brackets — 10% up to $2,550; 24% to $9,150; 35% to $12,500; 37% above

All amounts are **inflation-adjusted annually** using C-CPI-U (title26.md:6219). The above are 2018 base amounts; actual thresholds for any year come from IRS Revenue Procedures (title26.md:6352).

**Capital gains rates** — §1(h) (title26.md:5703):
- **0%** on adjusted net capital gain up to: MFJ $77,200 / HoH $51,700 / Single $38,600 (title26.md:6259)
- **15%** up to: MFJ $479,000 / HoH $452,400 / Single $425,800 (title26.md:6274)
- **20%** above those amounts
- **25%** on unrecaptured §1250 gain (depreciation recapture on real property) (title26.md:5748)
- **28%** on collectibles gain and §1202 gain (title26.md:5760)
- **Qualified dividends** taxed at the same preferential rates as capital gains (title26.md:5895)

**Kiddie tax** — §1(g) (title26.md:5520): Children under 18 (or under 24 if student whose earned income ≤ half their support) have unearned income taxed at parent's marginal rate

### §2 — Filing Status Definitions (title26.md:10051)

- **Surviving spouse** (§2(a)) (title26.md:10053): Spouse died in one of the 2 preceding tax years; taxpayer maintains household for dependent child; furnishes over half the cost; has not remarried
- **Head of household** (§2(b)) (title26.md:10107): Unmarried (not surviving spouse); maintains household as principal place of abode of a qualifying child (>½ year) or dependent; OR maintains separate household for parent; furnishes over half the cost
- **Single**: Not married (per §7703), not surviving spouse, not head of household (title26.md:5331)
- **Married filing jointly**: Married per §7703, files joint return under §6013 (title26.md:5298)
- **Married filing separately**: Married per §7703, does not file joint return (title26.md:5349)
- Legally separated under divorce/maintenance decree = not married (title26.md:10146)
- Nonresident alien spouse at any time = not married (title26.md:10150)
- Certain abandoned spouses maintaining home for child may file as HoH (§7703(b)) (title26.md:10173)

### §3 — Tax Tables (title26.md:10363)

- Secretary publishes simplified tax tables for individuals who don't itemize and have taxable income ≤ ceiling amount (at least $20,000) (title26.md:10367)
- Tax table tax treated as imposed by §1 for all purposes (title26.md:10406)
- Does not apply to short-year returns or estates/trusts (title26.md:10396)

## 2. Gross Income, AGI, and Taxable Income

### §61 — Gross Income Defined (title26.md:70846)

Gross income means **all income from whatever source derived**, including but not limited to:

1. Compensation for services (fees, commissions, fringe benefits) (title26.md:70854)
2. Gross income from business (title26.md:70857)
3. Gains from dealings in property (title26.md:70859)
4. Interest (title26.md:70861)
5. Rents (title26.md:70863)
6. Royalties (title26.md:70865)
7. Dividends (title26.md:70867)
8. Annuities (title26.md:70869)
9. Income from life insurance and endowment contracts (title26.md:70871)
10. Pensions (title26.md:70873)
11. Income from discharge of indebtedness (title26.md:70875)
12. Distributive share of partnership gross income (title26.md:70877)
13. Income in respect of a decedent (title26.md:70879)
14. Income from an interest in an estate or trust (title26.md:70881)

The list is explicitly **non-exhaustive** (title26.md:70851). Alimony was removed from gross income by the TCJA for instruments executed after Dec. 31, 2018 (title26.md:70897).

### §62 — Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) Defined (title26.md:71233)

AGI = Gross income minus the following **above-the-line deductions**:

- Trade/business deductions (not as employee) (title26.md:71241)
- Reimbursed employee expenses (title26.md:71250)
- Educator expenses — up to **$250** for qualified K-12 teachers (title26.md:71272)
- Performing artist expenses (AGI ≤ $16,000, 2+ employers) (title26.md:71259)
- Losses from sale of property (title26.md:71300)
- Deductions for rents and royalties (title26.md:71305)
- Self-employed retirement plan contributions (§404) (title26.md:71319)
- **IRA deduction** (§219) (title26.md:71324)
- Penalties for early withdrawal from savings (title26.md:71331)
- Reforestation expenses (title26.md:71343)
- Jury duty pay remitted to employer (title26.md:71356)
- Moving expenses (§217) (title26.md:71367)
- Archer MSA deduction (§220) (title26.md:71371)
- **Student loan interest** (§221) (title26.md:71375)
- **HSA deduction** (§223) (title26.md:71381)
- Costs of discrimination suits (title26.md:71385)
- Whistleblower attorney fees (title26.md:71398)

Note: The §199A qualified business income deduction is **not** above-the-line (title26.md:71425).

### §63 — Taxable Income Defined (title26.md:72402)

Taxable income = AGI minus the **standard deduction** (or itemized deductions) minus personal exemptions and certain other deductions.

**Standard Deduction** (post-2017, made permanent) (title26.md:72514):
- **Single / Married Filing Separately**: $15,750 (title26.md:72525)
- **Head of Household**: $23,625 (title26.md:72522)
- **Married Filing Jointly / Surviving Spouse**: $31,500 (title26.md:72449, 72525)
- Inflation-adjusted for years after 2025 (title26.md:72536)

**Additional Standard Deduction** for age 65+ or blind (title26.md:72598):
- **$600** per qualifying condition for married taxpayers (title26.md:72602)
- **$750** per qualifying condition for unmarried taxpayers (title26.md:72627)

**Dependents**: basic standard deduction limited to the greater of $500 or $250 + earned income (title26.md:72493)

**Cannot use standard deduction**: married filing separately when spouse itemizes, nonresident aliens, short-year filers (title26.md:72497)

## 3. Items Included in Gross Income

### §85 — Unemployment Compensation (title26.md:80444)

- **All unemployment compensation is included in gross income** (title26.md:80448)
- Special 2020 rule: first $10,200 excluded if AGI < $150,000 (title26.md:80461)

### §86 — Social Security Benefits (title26.md:80658)

- Up to **50%** of benefits taxable if modified AGI + ½ of benefits exceeds **base amount** (title26.md:80664)
- Up to **85%** taxable if exceeds **adjusted base amount** (title26.md:80676)
- **Base amounts**: $25,000 single, $32,000 joint, $0 married filing separately (not living apart) (title26.md:80727)
- **Adjusted base amounts**: $34,000 single, $44,000 joint (title26.md:80743)

### §72 — Annuities; Certain Proceeds of Endowment and Life Insurance Contracts (title26.md:74051)

- **Exclusion ratio**: Portion of annuity payment excluded = investment in contract / expected return (title26.md:74051)
- Once full investment is recovered, all payments are fully taxable
- **Simplified method** for qualified employer plans — anticipated payments by age (title26.md:74051):
  - 55 or under: 360; 55–60: 310; 60–65: 260; 65–70: 210; over 70: 160
- **Plan loans** (§72(p)): Not treated as distributions if ≤ lesser of $50,000 or ½ of vested benefit (min $10,000); must repay within 5 years (except principal residence)
- **10% early distribution penalty** (§72(t), title26.md:75384): Applies before age 59½; exceptions for death, disability, substantially equal periodic payments, separation after age 55, medical expenses, QDRO, higher education (IRAs), first-time homebuyer (IRAs up to $10,000), qualified birth/adoption (up to $5,000)

### §74 — Prizes and Awards (title26.md:75794)

- Prizes and awards are **included in gross income** (title26.md:75794)
- **Exception**: Awards for religious, charitable, scientific, educational, artistic, literary, or civic achievement — if recipient was selected without action on their part, and assigns the award to a governmental unit or charity

### §83 — Property Transferred in Connection with Performance of Services (title26.md:78996)

- Property (e.g., restricted stock) received for services is taxed when **substantially vested** (no longer subject to substantial risk of forfeiture)
- Amount included = FMV at vesting minus amount paid
- **§83(b) election**: Taxpayer may elect to include in income at time of transfer (before vesting), locking in lower value; if property is later forfeited, no deduction allowed

## 4. Items Excluded from Gross Income

### §102 — Gifts and Inheritances (title26.md:83032)

- Value of property acquired by gift, bequest, devise, or inheritance is **excluded** from gross income (title26.md:83036)
- Income **from** gifted/inherited property is still taxable (title26.md:83041)
- Transfers from employer to employee are **not** excluded (title26.md:83061)

### §121 — Exclusion of Gain from Sale of Principal Residence (title26.md:90818)

- Exclude up to **$250,000** gain ($500,000 for joint filers) from sale of principal residence (title26.md:90832, 90841)
- Must have owned and used as principal residence for 2 of the last 5 years (title26.md:90822)
- Can only use once every **2 years** (title26.md:90864)
- Surviving spouse: $500,000 exclusion available for sales within 2 years of spouse's death (title26.md:90871)
- Gain attributable to depreciation (post May 6, 1997) is **not** excluded (title26.md:91061)
- Partial exclusion available for sales due to change in employment, health, or unforeseen circumstances (title26.md:90960)
- Nursing home residents: 1 year of use in 5-year period sufficient if physically/mentally incapable of self-care (title26.md:91067)
- Military/Foreign Service/intelligence community: 5-year period suspended during extended duty (title26.md:91104)

### §101 — Life Insurance Proceeds Exclusion (title26.md:81984)

- Life insurance proceeds received by reason of death are **excluded from gross income** (title26.md:81984)
- **Transfer-for-value exception**: If policy was transferred for consideration, exclusion limited to consideration paid plus premiums; exceptions for transfers with carryover basis, to the insured, a partner, or a corporation of the insured
- Interest on proceeds held by insurer IS taxable (title26.md:81984)

### §103 — Interest on State and Local Bonds (title26.md:83090)

- Interest on state/local (municipal) bonds is **excluded from gross income** (title26.md:83090)
- **Exception**: Private activity bonds (unless qualified), arbitrage bonds, unregistered bonds
- Note: Municipal bond interest IS included in modified AGI for Social Security benefit taxation (§86) and may trigger AMT for private activity bonds

### §104 — Compensation for Injuries or Sickness (title26.md:85183)

- **Excluded**: Workers' compensation, damages for **physical** injuries/sickness (not punitive damages), accident/health insurance payments, military disability pensions (title26.md:85183)
- **Emotional distress** alone is NOT a physical injury — but medical care costs for emotional distress ARE excludable
- **Punitive damages**: Generally taxable (exception for wrongful death in certain states)

### §108 — Income from Discharge of Indebtedness (title26.md:86700)

- Cancelled debt is generally gross income, but **excluded** if discharge occurs in:
  - (A) Title 11 bankruptcy (title26.md:86700)
  - (B) Insolvency (to extent of insolvency)
  - (C) Qualified farm indebtedness
  - (D) Qualified real property business indebtedness
  - (E) **Qualified principal residence indebtedness** — discharged before **January 1, 2026** (title26.md:86700)
- **Student loan discharge** (§108(f)): Excluded if pursuant to work-in-certain-professions requirement, NHSC loan repayment, or state loan repayment programs
- Exclusion requires **tax attribute reduction** (NOLs, credits, capital losses, basis) (title26.md:86700)

### §117 — Qualified Scholarships (title26.md:89656)

- Amounts received as a qualified scholarship by a **degree candidate** are excluded (title26.md:89656)
- **Qualified**: Tuition, fees, books, supplies, and equipment required for courses
- **Not excluded**: Amounts for room, board, or payment for teaching/research services (with exceptions for NHSC and Armed Forces Health Professions programs)
- **Qualified tuition reduction** (§117(d)): Excluded for employees of educational organizations

### §135 — U.S. Savings Bonds for Higher Education (title26.md:90338)

- Interest from Series EE/I bonds redeemed to pay qualified higher education expenses may be **excluded** (title26.md:90338)
- **Income phase-out** applies (inflation-adjusted)
- Bond must be issued after 1989; owner must be at least 24 at issuance

## 5. Personal Exemptions and Dependents

### §151 — Personal Exemptions (title26.md:112329)

- Base exemption amount: $2,000 (inflation-adjusted) (title26.md:112358)
- **For tax years after 2017**: exemption amount is **zero** (permanently, per 2025 amendments) (title26.md:112413)
- **New deduction for seniors** (tax years before 2029): $6,000 per qualified individual age 65+ (title26.md:112427)
  - Phases out: reduced by 6% of MAGI over $75,000 ($150,000 joint) (title26.md:112446)
  - Must file jointly if married (title26.md:112471)

### §152 — Dependent Defined (title26.md:112887)

**Qualifying child** (title26.md:112940):
- Must bear relationship (child, sibling, or descendant thereof) (title26.md:112964)
- Same principal place of abode for more than half the year (title26.md:112948)
- Under age 19, or under 24 if a full-time student, or permanently disabled (title26.md:112980)
- Did not provide more than half their own support (title26.md:112953)

**Qualifying relative** (title26.md:113034):
- Relationship test: broad list of relatives, or any member of the household (title26.md:113056)
- Gross income less than the exemption amount (title26.md:113040)
- Taxpayer provides over half of support (title26.md:113044)
- Not a qualifying child of anyone (title26.md:113048)

**Tiebreaker rules**: when 2+ taxpayers can claim the same child, the parent takes priority, then the parent with whom child lived longest, then highest AGI (title26.md:112997)

## 6. Key Itemized Deductions

### §163 — Interest Deduction (title26.md:116508)

- General rule: all interest paid on indebtedness is deductible (title26.md:116512)
- **Qualified residence interest** (§163(h)): mortgage interest on acquisition indebtedness up to $750,000 ($375,000 MFS) for post-Dec 15, 2017 debt (title26.md:116508+)
- **Investment interest**: deductible only up to net investment income; excess carries forward (title26.md:116555)
- **Personal interest** (credit cards, personal loans): **not deductible** (title26.md:116508+)

### §164 — State and Local Tax (SALT) Deduction (title26.md:119300)

Deductible taxes:
- State/local/foreign real property taxes (title26.md:119308)
- State/local personal property taxes (title26.md:119310)
- State/local/foreign income taxes (or election to deduct **sales taxes** instead) (title26.md:119312, 119376)

**SALT cap (2018–2025)** (title26.md:119469):
- Aggregate deduction for state/local property, income, and sales taxes limited
- **2025**: $40,000 cap ($20,000 MFS) (title26.md:119501)
- **2026**: $40,400 cap (title26.md:119503)
- **2027–2029**: 101% of prior year cap (title26.md:119507)
- **After 2029**: reverts to $10,000 (title26.md:119512)
- **Phase-down for high earners** (before 2030): cap reduced by 30% of MAGI over threshold — $500,000 for 2025 (title26.md:119531), $505,000 for 2026 (title26.md:119534), 101% of prior year thereafter ($250,000 MFS for 2025), but not below $10,000 (title26.md:119518, 119543)
- Foreign real property taxes **not** deductible for 2018–2025 (title26.md:119474)
- Trade/business taxes are **not** subject to the cap (title26.md:119483)

### §170 — Charitable Contributions (title26.md:132628)

- **Cash contributions** to public charities: up to **50%** of contribution base (AGI) (title26.md:132738)
- **Cash to other organizations**: up to **30%** of contribution base (title26.md:132748)
- **Capital gain property** to public charities: up to **30%** of contribution base (title26.md:132774)
- **Capital gain property** to other organizations: up to **20%** (title26.md:132818)
- Excess contributions carry forward **5 years** (title26.md:132765)
- Contributions must be verified/substantiated per regulations (title26.md:132637)

### §213 — Medical and Dental Expenses (title26.md:147452)

- Deductible to the extent expenses exceed **7.5% of AGI** (title26.md:147461)
- Covers taxpayer, spouse, and dependents (title26.md:147458)
- **Medical care** includes: diagnosis, cure, treatment, prevention; transportation for medical care; long-term care services; health insurance premiums (title26.md:147497)
- Prescription drugs and insulin only (title26.md:147465)
- Cosmetic surgery generally **not** deductible unless for congenital abnormality, injury, or disease (title26.md:147580)
- Lodging away from home for medical care: up to $50/night (title26.md:147531)

## 7. Business, Loss, and Other Important Deductions

### §162 — Trade or Business Expenses (title26.md:114102)

- Deduction for all **ordinary and necessary expenses** in carrying on a trade or business (title26.md:114102)
- Includes: reasonable compensation, travel expenses (meals/lodging while away from home, not lavish), rentals
- **Temporary away from home**: Employment exceeding **1 year** is not temporary (no travel deduction)
- **Disallowed**: Illegal bribes/kickbacks, lobbying expenses (de minimis exception: $2,000 in-house), fines/penalties, sexual harassment settlements with NDAs
- **Meal deduction**: Limited to 50% under §274

### §165 — Losses (title26.md:120131)

- Individuals may deduct losses from: (1) trade or business, (2) transactions entered into for profit, (3) **casualty and theft** losses (title26.md:120131)
- **Casualty loss floors**: Each event reduced by $500; net personal casualty losses deductible only to extent exceeding **10% of AGI**
- **Post-2017**: Personal casualty losses allowed **only if attributable to a federally or state declared disaster** (exception if personal casualty gains exist) (title26.md:120131)
- **Wagering losses**: Limited to **90%** of wagering losses, and only to extent of wagering gains
- Capital losses governed by §1211/§1212

### §172 — Net Operating Loss Deduction (title26.md:137738)

- Post-2017 NOLs limited to **80% of taxable income** (computed without regard to NOL deduction, §199A, and §250) (title26.md:137738)
- **Carryforward**: Pre-2018 NOLs carry forward 20 years; post-2017 NOLs carry forward **indefinitely**
- **Carryback**: Generally **no carryback** for post-2017 NOLs (exception: farming losses get 2-year carryback)
- Election to waive carryback available

### §199A — Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction (title26.md:146332)

- Deduction for non-corporate taxpayers equal to the lesser of (title26.md:146336):
  - Combined QBI amount, or
  - 20% of (taxable income minus net capital gain)
- **20% of QBI** from each qualified trade or business (title26.md:146370)
- Includes 20% of qualified REIT dividends and publicly traded partnership income (title26.md:146361)
- **W-2 wage/property limitation** applies above threshold amount: lesser of 20% QBI or the greater of 50% of W-2 wages or 25% of W-2 wages + 2.5% of qualified property basis (title26.md:146373)
- **Threshold**: phases in over $75,000 ($150,000 joint) above threshold amount (title26.md:146396)
- Not treated as an above-the-line deduction; taken separately from AGI computation (title26.md:71425)

### §219 — IRA Deduction (title26.md:149106)

- Deductible amount: **$5,000** (base, inflation-adjusted) (title26.md:149155)
- **Catch-up** for age 50+: additional **$1,000** (inflation-adjusted starting 2024) (title26.md:149167, 149191)
- **Spousal IRA**: non-working spouse can contribute based on working spouse's compensation (title26.md:149206)
- **Phase-out for active participants** in employer plans: IRA deduction reduced as AGI exceeds applicable dollar amount over a $10,000 range ($20,000 for joint returns) (title26.md:149371)
- Contributions must be made by tax filing deadline (not including extensions) (title26.md:149306)

### §221 — Student Loan Interest Deduction (title26.md:151853)

- Above-the-line deduction of up to **$2,500** for interest on qualified education loans (title26.md:151853)
- **Phase-out** (base amounts, inflation-adjusted): begins at MAGI $50,000 ($100,000 joint), complete at $65,000 ($130,000 joint)
- Must file jointly if married; not available if claimed as a dependent
- Includes refinancing loans; excludes loans from related persons or employer plans

### §223 — Health Savings Accounts (title26.md:152303)

- **Annual contribution limits** (base amounts, inflation-adjusted): self-only **$2,250**; family **$4,500** (title26.md:152303)
- **Catch-up** (age 55+): additional **$1,000**/year
- **HDHP requirements** (base amounts): minimum deductible $1,000 self-only / $2,000 family; max OOP $5,000 / $10,000
- Distributions for qualified medical expenses are **tax-free**; non-qualified distributions are income + **20% additional tax** (exceptions: disability, death, Medicare age)
- Bronze and catastrophic plans treated as HDHPs
- Contribution limit is zero once Medicare-eligible
- Above-the-line deduction

### §224 — Qualified Tips Deduction (title26.md:153381)

- Above-the-line deduction for qualified tips, capped at **$25,000**/year (title26.md:153381)
- **Phase-out**: reduced by $100 per $1,000 of MAGI over $150,000 ($300,000 joint)
- Qualified tips: cash tips in occupations that customarily received tips as of Dec. 31, 2024 (per Secretary's published list)
- Must not be in a specified service trade or business (§199A(d)(2))
- Must file jointly if married; SSN required
- **Effective**: Tax years beginning after Dec. 31, 2024; **sunsets Dec. 31, 2028**
- Added by Pub. L. 119–21 (July 4, 2025)

### §225 — Qualified Overtime Compensation Deduction (title26.md:153525)

- Above-the-line deduction for qualified overtime pay, capped at **$12,500** ($25,000 joint) (title26.md:153525)
- **Phase-out**: reduced by $100 per $1,000 of MAGI over $150,000 ($300,000 joint)
- Qualified overtime: pay required under FLSA §7 exceeding the regular rate (excludes qualified tips)
- Must file jointly if married; SSN required
- **Effective**: Tax years beginning after Dec. 31, 2024; **sunsets Dec. 31, 2028**
- Added by Pub. L. 119–21 (July 4, 2025)

### §280A — Business Use of Home / Vacation Rental (title26.md:164272)

- **General rule**: No deduction for dwelling used as a residence, except (title26.md:164272):
- **Home office**: Deductible if used **exclusively and regularly** as principal place of business (includes admin/management activities if no other fixed location), or as place to meet clients, or a separate structure for business
- For employees: must be for convenience of employer
- **Deduction limitation**: Cannot exceed gross income from the use minus allocable deductions allowed regardless of use; excess carries forward
- **"Used as residence" test**: Personal use exceeds the greater of 14 days or 10% of rental days
- **14-day rental exclusion** (§280A(g)): If residence rented **fewer than 15 days**, rental income is **completely excluded** (no rental expense deductions either)

## 8. Miscellaneous Itemized Deductions

### §67 — 2% Floor on Miscellaneous Itemized Deductions (title26.md:73390)

- Miscellaneous itemized deductions allowed only to the extent exceeding 2% of AGI (title26.md:73392)
- Major deductions **NOT** subject to the floor: interest (§163), taxes (§164), charitable contributions (§170), medical expenses (§213), casualty losses (title26.md:73403)
- **Permanently suspended** for tax years after 2017 (per 2025 amendments) (title26.md:73550)

### §68 — Overall Limitation on Itemized Deductions (title26.md:73730)

- **Suspended for 2018–2025** (title26.md:73770)
- **Starting 2026**: new formula — itemized deductions reduced by 2/37 of the lesser of total itemized deductions or excess of taxable income over the 37% bracket threshold (title26.md:73738)

## 9. Tax Credits — Nonrefundable Personal Credits

### §21 — Child and Dependent Care Credit (title26.md:12007)

- Credit for employment-related expenses for care of qualifying individuals (children under 13, disabled dependents/spouse) (title26.md:12042)
- Credit percentage: starts at **50%**, phases down to 35% (AGI $15,000–$45,000), then to 20% (AGI $75,000/$150,000+) (title26.md:12023)
- Maximum qualifying expenses: **$3,000** (1 individual), **$6,000** (2+) (title26.md:12114)
- **Nonrefundable** (title26.md:12015)

### §22 — Credit for Elderly and Permanently Disabled (title26.md:12756)

- **15%** of "section 22 amount" (title26.md:12762)
- Initial amounts: $5,000 single, $7,500 joint (both qualifying), $3,750 MFS (title26.md:12791)
- Reduced by nontaxable Social Security/VA benefits (title26.md:12835)
- Phase-out: reduced by 50% of AGI over $7,500 single / $10,000 joint (title26.md:12865)

### §23 — Adoption Expenses Credit (title26.md:13227)

- Credit for qualified adoption expenses up to **$10,000** per adoption (title26.md:13271)
- Special needs adoptions: deemed $10,000 in expenses (title26.md:13252)
- Phase-out: AGI $150,000–$190,000 (title26.md:13279)
- **Partially refundable**: up to $5,000 (title26.md:13261)
- Unused nonrefundable portion carries forward 5 years (title26.md:13323)

### §24 — Child Tax Credit (title26.md:13963)

- **$2,200** per qualifying child under age 17 (post-2017 per subsection (h)) (title26.md:14148)
- **$500** credit for other dependents (nonrefundable) (title26.md:14162)
- **Phase-out**: $50 reduction per $1,000 of MAGI over $400,000 joint / $200,000 other (title26.md:14154)
- **Partially refundable** (Additional Child Tax Credit): up to **$1,400** per child (inflation-adjusted), equal to 15% of earned income over $2,500 (title26.md:14182, 14185)
- SSN required for both taxpayer and child (title26.md:14190)

### §25A — Education Credits (title26.md:16106)

**American Opportunity Tax Credit (AOTC)**:
- 100% of first $2,000 + 25% of next $2,000 = max **$2,500** per student (title26.md:16124)
- First 4 years of postsecondary education only (title26.md:16136)
- **40% refundable** (up to $1,000) (title26.md:16422)

**Lifetime Learning Credit (LLC)**:
- 20% of up to $10,000 = max **$2,000** per taxpayer (title26.md:16217)
- No limit on years; any course to acquire/improve job skills (title26.md:16217)
- **Nonrefundable**

Both phase out: MAGI $80,000–$90,000 ($160,000–$180,000 joint) (title26.md:16243)

### §25B — Saver's Credit (title26.md:16825)

- Credit for retirement savings contributions (IRA, 401(k), etc.) up to **$2,000** (title26.md:16831)
- Credit percentage by AGI (joint): 50% (≤$30,000), 20% ($30,000–$32,500), 10% ($32,500–$50,000), 0% (>$50,000) (title26.md:16839)
- Head of household: 75% of joint thresholds; single/MFS: 50% (title26.md:16859)
- Must be age 18+, not a dependent, not a full-time student (title26.md:16892)
- **Nonrefundable**

### §25C — Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit (title26.md:17281)

- **30%** of qualifying expenditures (insulation, windows, doors, heat pumps, etc.) (title26.md:17287)
- Annual caps: $1,200 overall, $600 per item, $2,000 for heat pumps/biomass (title26.md:17302)
- $150 cap for home energy audits (title26.md:17338)
- No income phase-out
- **Does not apply after Dec. 31, 2025** (title26.md:17591)

### §25D — Residential Clean Energy Credit (title26.md:18115)

- **30%** of expenditures for solar, wind, geothermal, fuel cell, battery storage (title26.md:18347)
- No dollar cap (except fuel cell: $500 per half kW) (title26.md:18143)
- No income phase-out
- Unused credit carries forward (title26.md:18156)
- **Does not apply after Dec. 31, 2025** (title26.md:18362)

### §25E — Previously-Owned Clean Vehicle Credit (title26.md:18698)

- Credit = lesser of **$4,000** or **30%** of sale price (title26.md:18698)
- Vehicle must cost **$25,000 or less**; model year at least 2 years older than calendar year
- **MAGI cutoff** (hard, not gradual): $150,000 joint / $112,500 HoH / $75,000 other (uses lesser of current or prior year MAGI)
- One credit per buyer per 3-year period; must be acquired from a dealer
- Transfer of credit to dealer allowed; VIN required on return
- **Terminates September 30, 2025** (accelerated from Dec. 31, 2032 by Pub. L. 119–21)

### §27 — Foreign Tax Credit (title26.md:19867)

- Credit for income taxes paid to foreign countries and U.S. possessions, per §901 (title26.md:19867)
- Substantive rules (limitations, carryback/carryforward) in §§901–909
- Relevant for individuals with foreign-source income (foreign investments, working abroad)

### §30D — Clean Vehicle Credit (title26.md:21693)

- Up to **$7,500** total: $3,750 for critical minerals requirements + $3,750 for battery component requirements (title26.md:21693)
- **MAGI cutoff** (hard): $300,000 joint / $225,000 HoH / $150,000 other (uses lesser of current or prior year)
- **MSRP limits**: $80,000 for vans/SUVs/pickups; $55,000 all others
- Vehicle requirements: new, qualified manufacturer, GVWR <14,000 lbs, electric motor, battery ≥7 kWh, final assembly in North America
- Transfer of credit to dealer allowed
- **Terminates September 30, 2025** (accelerated from Dec. 31, 2032 by Pub. L. 119–21)

## 10. Tax Credits — Refundable Credits

### §31 — Tax Withheld on Wages (title26.md:22615)

- Dollar-for-dollar credit for federal income tax withheld from wages (title26.md:22621)
- Also covers backup withholding and Social Security overpayment refunds (title26.md:22636)

### §32 — Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) (title26.md:22751)

- **Refundable credit** for low/moderate-income workers (title26.md:22757)
- Credit = credit percentage × earned income (up to earned income amount), minus phaseout percentage × (AGI or earned income, whichever greater) over phaseout amount (title26.md:22757)

**Credit/phaseout percentages** (base amounts, inflation-adjusted) (title26.md:22783):

| Qualifying Children | Credit % | Phaseout % |
|---------------------|----------|------------|
| 3 or more           | 45%      | 21.06%     |
| 2                   | 40%      | 21.06%     |
| 1                   | 34%      | 15.98%     |
| 0                   | 7.65%    | 7.65%      |

**Earned income / phaseout amounts** (base) (title26.md:22805):
- 1 child: $6,330 / $11,610
- 2+ children: $8,890 / $11,610
- No children: $4,220 / $5,280
- Joint filers: phaseout amount increased by $5,000 (title26.md:22819)

**Key eligibility rules**:
- Must have SSN for taxpayer, spouse, and qualifying children (title26.md:22872)
- **Investment income** cannot exceed $10,000 (inflation-adjusted) (title26.md:23033)
- No qualifying children: must be age 25–64, US abode for >½ year, not a dependent (title26.md:22835)
- Married couples must file jointly (with separated spouse exception) (title26.md:22969)
- 10-year disqualification for fraud; 2-year for reckless/intentional disregard (title26.md:23119)

### §35 — Health Insurance Costs Credit (title26.md:25046)

- **72.5%** of qualified health insurance premiums (title26.md:25051)
- For eligible TAA recipients, alternative TAA recipients, and PBGC pension recipients age 55+ (title26.md:25090)
- No income limit
- Coverage months must begin before Jan. 1, 2022 (title26.md:25076)

### §36B — Premium Tax Credit (ACA Marketplace) (title26.md:26647)

- **Refundable credit** for insurance purchased through ACA Exchange (title26.md:26651)
- Available if household income is 100%–400% of federal poverty line (permanent rules) (title26.md:26847)
- **2021–2025 temporary rules**: 400% cap removed; more generous percentages; households under 150% FPL pay 0% (title26.md:26741, 26868)
- Credit = lesser of actual premium or (benchmark silver plan premium minus expected contribution) (title26.md:26669)
- Advance payments must be reconciled on the return; excess repaid (title26.md:27160)
- Employer coverage interaction: employee can claim PTC if employer plan requires >9.5% of household income or provides <60% minimum value (title26.md:26914)

## 11. Capital Gains and Losses

### §1001 — Determination of Gain or Loss (title26.md:339459)

- **Gain** = amount realized minus adjusted basis; **Loss** = adjusted basis minus amount realized (title26.md:339459)
- **Amount realized** = money received + FMV of property received
- Entire gain/loss recognized unless otherwise provided (§1031, §1033, §1041, etc.)

### §1014 — Basis of Property Acquired from a Decedent (Stepped-Up Basis) (title26.md:339953)

- Basis = **fair market value at date of death** (or alternate valuation date under §2032) (title26.md:339953)
- Applies to bequests, devises, inheritance, revocable trusts, community property
- **Does NOT apply to**: annuities (§72), income in respect of a decedent (§691)
- **Anti-abuse**: Appreciated property gifted to decedent within 1 year of death that passes back to donor — basis is decedent's adjusted basis (not FMV)
- Basis cannot exceed estate tax value (§1014(f))

### §1015 — Basis of Property Acquired by Gift (title26.md:340384)

- **For gain**: Basis = donor's adjusted basis (carryover basis) (title26.md:340384)
- **For loss**: If donor's basis exceeds FMV at time of gift, basis = FMV (creates potential "no gain/no loss" zone)
- Basis increased (but not above FMV) by gift tax paid attributable to net appreciation

### §1031 — Like-Kind Exchanges (title26.md:342673)

- No gain/loss recognized on exchange of **real property** held for business/investment for like-kind real property (title26.md:342673)
- **Limited to real property only** since TCJA 2017 (no personal property, equipment, vehicles)
- **Identification period**: Replacement property identified within **45 days** of transfer
- **Exchange period**: Replacement received within **180 days** (or tax return due date if earlier)
- **Boot**: If money/non-like-kind property received, gain recognized to extent of boot
- **Related parties**: If either party disposes within **2 years**, nonrecognition revoked (exceptions for death, involuntary conversion)
- US and foreign real property are NOT like-kind

### §1041 — Transfers Between Spouses or Incident to Divorce (title26.md:344874)

- **No gain or loss** on transfer to spouse or former spouse incident to divorce (title26.md:344874)
- Treated as gift: transferee takes transferor's adjusted basis (carryover basis)
- **"Incident to divorce"**: Within 1 year after marriage ceases, or related to cessation of marriage
- Does NOT apply if spouse/former spouse is a nonresident alien

### §1221 — Capital Asset Defined (title26.md:350914)

Capital asset = all property held by the taxpayer **except** (title26.md:350918):
- Inventory / stock in trade (title26.md:350922)
- Depreciable/real property used in trade or business (title26.md:350928)
- Self-created intellectual property (patents, copyrights, etc.) (title26.md:350933)
- Accounts/notes receivable from business (title26.md:350946)
- Government publications received free (title26.md:350950)
- Hedging transactions, commodities derivatives, business supplies (title26.md:350962)

### §1211 — Limitation on Capital Losses (title26.md:350175)

- Individuals: capital losses deductible against capital gains plus up to **$3,000** ($1,500 MFS) of ordinary income (title26.md:350190)

### §1212 — Capital Loss Carryover (title26.md:350269)

- Individuals: excess capital losses carry forward indefinitely (title26.md:350269+)

### §1222 — Capital Gains and Losses Terms (title26.md:351190)

- **Short-term**: Capital asset held **not more than 1 year** (title26.md:351190)
- **Long-term**: Capital asset held **more than 1 year**
- **Net capital gain** = net long-term capital gain exceeding net short-term capital loss
- Actual tax rates (0%/15%/20%/25%/28%) are in §1(h), not here — §1222 is definitional

## 12. Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT)

### §55 — AMT Imposed (title26.md:64924)

- AMT = tentative minimum tax minus regular tax (title26.md:64928)
- **AMT rates**: 26% on first $175,000 of taxable excess, 28% above ($87,500 for MFS) (title26.md:64948)
- Capital gains taxed at preferential rates under AMT as well (title26.md:65004)

**Exemption amounts** (base, inflation-adjusted) (title26.md:65077):
- Joint / surviving spouse: **$78,750** (title26.md:65077)
- Single: **$50,600** (title26.md:65083)
- MFS: 50% of joint amount (title26.md:65089)
- Exemption phased out at 25% of AMTI over $150,000 joint / $112,500 single (title26.md:65100)

## 13. Self-Employment Tax

### §1401 — Self-Employment Tax Rate (title26.md:377596)

- **OASDI**: 12.4% of self-employment income (title26.md:377602)
- **Hospital Insurance (Medicare)**: 2.9% of self-employment income (title26.md:377611)
- **Additional Medicare**: 0.9% on SE income exceeding $250,000 joint / $200,000 single / $125,000 MFS (title26.md:377621)
- Total SE tax rate: **15.3%** (12.4% + 2.9%) on income up to the Social Security wage base, 2.9%+ above

## 14. Retirement Accounts

### §408A — Roth IRAs (title26.md:206057)

- **No deduction** for Roth IRA contributions (title26.md:206057)
- Contribution limit: Same as traditional IRA (§219); minus contributions to other IRAs
- **MAGI phase-out** for contributions (base amounts, inflation-adjusted): joint $150,000 over $10,000 range; single/HoH $95,000 over $15,000 range; MFS $0 over $10,000 range
- **Qualified distributions** (tax-free): After age 59½, death, disability, or qualified special purpose — AND after **5-taxable-year period** from first Roth contribution year
- **No required minimum distributions** during owner's lifetime
- **Ordering**: Distributions come first from contributions (tax-free), then conversions (FIFO), then earnings
- **Conversions**: Traditional-to-Roth included in income (no 10% penalty); converted amounts distributed within 5 years may be subject to §72(t) penalty
- **529-to-Roth rollover**: From 529 plans maintained 15+ years, subject to IRA contribution limits and **$35,000** lifetime cap

### §529 — Qualified Tuition Programs (title26.md:263347)

- Earnings grow **tax-free**; distributions for qualified education expenses are **tax-free** (title26.md:263347)
- **Qualified expenses**: Tuition, fees, books, supplies, equipment, room and board, computer technology
- **K-12 tuition**: Expanded to include elementary/secondary tuition, curriculum materials, tutoring, testing fees, dual enrollment, educational therapies for disabilities
- **Student loan repayment**: Up to **$10,000** lifetime per beneficiary (including siblings)
- **Apprenticeship expenses**: Registered apprenticeship programs qualify
- **Gift tax**: Contributions are completed gifts; may elect to spread up to 5 years of annual gift exclusion
- **529-to-Roth IRA rollover**: From accounts maintained 15+ years, contributions made 5+ years ago, subject to annual IRA limit and **$35,000** lifetime cap
- **Penalty**: 10% on earnings portion of non-qualified distributions
- No income limits on contributions or use

## 15. Foreign Earned Income

### §911 — Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (title26.md:325314)

- Qualified individuals may **exclude** foreign earned income and housing cost amount (title26.md:325314)
- **Exclusion amount**: Base of **$80,000** (inflation-adjusted, base year 2004), computed daily
- **Qualified individual**: Tax home in a foreign country AND either:
  - Bona fide resident of foreign country for entire taxable year, OR
  - Present in foreign country for at least **330 full days** in any 12-month period
- **Housing cost amount**: Excess of housing expenses over 16% of the exclusion amount; limited to 30% of the exclusion amount (adjustable by Secretary for geographic differences)
- Excludable items do NOT include: pensions/annuities, U.S. government employee pay

## 16. Filing Requirements and Deadlines

### §6012 — Persons Required to File (title26.md:498203)

- Every individual with gross income ≥ exemption amount must file, with exceptions (title26.md:498210)
- **For 2018–2025**: no return required if gross income does not exceed the standard deduction (title26.md:498380)
- Joint filers: no return if combined gross income ≤ joint standard deduction (title26.md:498390)
- Must report tax-exempt interest on the return (title26.md:498367)
- Gross income computed **without regard to** §121 (home sale exclusion) and §911 (foreign earned income exclusion) (title26.md:498359)

### §6072 — Filing Deadline (title26.md:516367)

- Calendar-year individuals: **April 15** following the close of the calendar year (title26.md:516373)
- Fiscal-year filers: 15th day of the 4th month after close of fiscal year (title26.md:516375)

---

### §6081 — Extension of Time for Filing Returns (title26.md:516778)

- Secretary may grant reasonable extension, not exceeding **6 months** (except taxpayers abroad) (title26.md:516778)
- Individuals: 6-month automatic extension available (Form 4868)
- **Extension to file is NOT an extension to pay** — see §6151

### §6151 — Time and Place for Paying Tax (title26.md:527517)

- Tax must be paid at the time fixed for filing the return, **without regard to any extension of time for filing** (title26.md:527517)
- Even with a filing extension, **tax is due on the original due date** (April 15 for calendar-year individuals)

## 17. Penalties

### §6651 — Failure to File or Pay (title26.md:564080)

- **Failure to file**: 5% of tax due per month (or fraction), max **25%** (title26.md:564095)
- **Failure to pay**: 0.5% of tax due per month, max **25%** (title26.md:564105)
- Minimum penalty for returns filed >60 days late: lesser of **$435** or 100% of tax due (title26.md:564127)
- Penalties apply unless failure is due to reasonable cause (not willful neglect) (title26.md:564092)
- Failure-to-file penalty reduced by failure-to-pay penalty for overlapping months (title26.md:564154)

### §6654 — Failure to Pay Estimated Income Tax (title26.md:566326)

- Penalty at underpayment rate (§6621) on underpayment amount for the underpayment period (title26.md:566326)
- **4 installments** due: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- **Required annual payment** = lesser of: **90%** of current year tax, or **100%** of prior year tax (**110%** if prior year AGI > $150,000 / $75,000 MFS)
- **No penalty if**: Tax owed (after withholding) < **$1,000**; or no tax liability in prior year (12-month year, citizen/resident, return filed)
- **Annualized income installment method** available to reduce installments
- **Farmers/fishermen**: Only 1 installment (January 15); no penalty if return filed and tax paid by March 1

### §6662 — Accuracy-Related Penalty (title26.md:569596)

- **20%** of underpayment attributable to: negligence, substantial understatement, valuation misstatements, lack of economic substance, undisclosed foreign financial assets (title26.md:569596)
- **Substantial understatement** (individuals): greater of **10% of tax required** or **$5,000** (5% threshold if claiming §199A deduction)
- **Gross valuation misstatement**: penalty increases to **40%**
- Reduced for items with substantial authority or adequate disclosure with reasonable basis
